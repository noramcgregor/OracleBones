The OG ChatGPT: Oracle Bones

How the Shang Dynasty asked questions 3,000 years ago

Oracle bones are animal bones, usually ox shoulder bones or the underside of turtle shells that were used by Shang kings and priests (the diviners) to ask questions to the gods. They would carve their questions (about things like the weather, the future of the dynasty or military victory) onto the bone or shell using a sharp tool. A hot metal rod was applied until the bone or shell cracked. The diviners would then interpret the cracks and write the answer on the bone or shell.
Dating to the Shang dynasty (c. 1600 – 1050 BC), they are the oldest form of Chinese writing and are the oldest items held in the British Library.
