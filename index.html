Oracle Bones

How the Shang Dynasty asked questions 3,000 years ago

Dating to the Shang dynasty (c. 1600–1050 BC), these bones are the oldest items held in the British Library.

Oracle bones are animal bones, usually ox shoulder bones (like this one!) or the underside of turtle shells that were used by Shang kings and priests (the diviners) to ask questions to the gods.

They would carve their questions (about things like the weather, the future of the dynasty or military victory) onto the bone or shell using a sharp tool.

A hot metal rod was then applied to the bone or shell until it cracked. The diviners would then interpret the cracks and write the answer on the bone or shell.
